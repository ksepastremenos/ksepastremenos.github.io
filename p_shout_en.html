This project was the result of a conceptual preoccupation of mine concerning the privacy of expression in urban environments.

A thought that travels all the way from the need to vent and express oneself audibly, to elevated rent prices and poorly insulated, shared apartments. And how the lingering fears of precarity dictate our times like off-sync metronomes, deepening our alienation day by day.

The composition is meant to represent situations of spending time with oneself in locations of compressed privacy. Whether those arrive as a need or an obligation, the connecting factor is the contrast drawn between excessive isolation and insufficient privacy.

Rendered in Blender Cycles, composited in After Effects.
